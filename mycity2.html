London, Ontario

Overview

London is a big city in Southwestern Ontario, Canada (Named after London, England).
London was named in 1793, although the first European settlements wouldn't arrive until 1801. The idea that London would be the capital of Upper Canada (Upper Canada was the area that is now most of Ontario) was proposed, but was denied in favor of Toronto.

Population

According to a census held in 2016. the population of London is 383,822.

Economy

London's economy mainly comes from medical research, insurance, manufacturing, and information technology. Life sciences and biotechnology makes up for $1.5 billion of London's economy annually.

Education

London is home to the University of Western Ontario, which specializes in medicinal areas and attracts thousands of students from across Canada.
